How it works

Three roles, one visible chain of authority.

The person speaks. The companion helps organize. The consent engine determines what may move. The clinician interprets what arrives.

01 · PERSON

Reflect between appointments

A private place for a difficult night, a small win, a symptom timeline, or a question worth carrying into the next session.

02 · COMPANION

Organize without diagnosing

The system can propose themes and summaries, but must distinguish self-report, extracted text, and model inference.

03 · CLINICIAN

Read only the authorized view

The dashboard shows consent scope, provenance, limitations, and deliberate gaps—because absence can be a valid privacy choice.

Consent, by design

Nobody’s words move through an invisible door.

Care-Tech is built around a narrow rule: ordinary care information reaches the clinician view only through an active, visible sharing scope. Jointly authored safety plans are separately versioned, reviewable, and explicit about triggers, recipients, and exact disclosure.

The Silicari companion layer must also disclose its own operating limits: what came from the person, what was extracted, what was generated, and what requires human judgment.

Granular by defaultTopic, summary, transcript, and structured self-report can carry different scopes.
Visible and expiringEvery scope shows confirmation, review, version, and expiry metadata.

Trust boundaries

The model is not the authority.

A credible implementation separates conversational generation from consent enforcement and clinical decision-making.

MODEL

Proposes

Creates a reply, a summary candidate, labeled observations, and a possible safety-plan match with an evidence span.

CONSENT ENGINE

Governs

Checks scope, version, expiry, recipient, exact disclosure, and deterministic plan thresholds before anything may move.

HUMAN

Determines

Interprets meaning, makes clinical decisions, and remains accountable for care. The system never claims otherwise.
